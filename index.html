Meet the CMS-1500 Form

What is the CMS-1500?

The CMS-1500 (also called the HCFA-1500) is the standard paper claim form used to bill Medicare, Medicaid, and most commercial insurance carriers for professional (non-facility) services.

Current version: CMS-1500 (02/12)

Even though most claims are submitted electronically, the CMS-1500 is the visual standard that maps to the electronic 837P transaction. As a biller, you need to know every box.

The Three Sections

The form has 33 numbered boxes organized into three main areas:

Boxes 1–13
Patient & Insurance Info
Who is the patient? Who is paying?
Boxes 14–24
Clinical & Services
What happened? What was done?
Boxes 25–33
Provider & Billing
Who did it? Where? How to pay?

Patient & Insurance Blocks (1–13)

Where Does This Info Come From?

Blocks 1–13 come from two main sources:

📋 Patient registration/demographic form — name, DOB, address, etc.

🪪 Insurance card — plan name, ID number, group number, etc.

⚠️ Not everything on the insurance card goes on the claim. You must know what goes where.

Clinical & Service Blocks (14–24)

The Heart of the Claim

Boxes 14–24 tell the payer what happened clinically and what services were provided. This is where medical necessity lives.

Key Blocks in This Section

Box 14: Date of Current Illness/Injury +
Enter the date the patient's current condition started. Found in the provider's note or history. Use MM/DD/YYYY format.
Box 17: Referring Provider +
Name of the referring or ordering provider, if applicable. Not always required — only when another provider referred the patient.
Box 21: Diagnosis Codes (ICD-10-CM) +
Up to 12 diagnosis codes that justify the services. Listed with pointers A–L. These come from the provider's documentation.
Box 24: Service Lines +
The most important area. Each line includes: Date of Service (24A), Place of Service (24B), CPT/HCFA code (24D), Diagnosis Pointer (24E), Charges (24F), Units (24G).

📄 Sample Provider Note Excerpt

Date of Service: 03/10/2025

Chief Complaint: Sore throat and fever for 3 days

History: Patient also has a history of hypertension (controlled) and seasonal allergies. Smokes 1 pack/day.

Assessment: Acute pharyngitis (J02.9)

Plan: Rapid strep test performed. Prescribed amoxicillin. Follow up in 1 week if no improvement.

Procedure: Office visit, established patient, level 3 (99213). Rapid strep test (87880).

Place of Service: Office (11)

Charges & Totals (Box 24F / 28)

Getting the Money Right

Each service line in Box 24 has a charge amount in Box 24F. Box 28 is the total of all charges. Getting this wrong = claim issues.

Provider & Billing Blocks (25–33)

Who Did the Work?

Boxes 25–33 identify the provider, practice, and billing entity. These come from the provider's office records, not the patient.

Key Blocks

Box 25: Federal Tax ID / EIN +
The provider's or practice's federal tax ID number. Usually an EIN (Employer Identification Number). Check the box for SSN or EIN.
Box 26: Patient Account Number +
Optional. The practice's internal account number for the patient. Helps match the remittance to the patient.
Box 27: Accept Assignment +
Does the provider accept the insurance's allowed amount as payment in full? "Yes" for participating providers.
Box 31: Signature of Provider +
The rendering provider's signature and date. In electronic claims, "Signature on File" is often used.
Box 32: Service Facility Location +
The name and address of where the service was performed. May differ from the billing address.
Box 33: Billing Provider Info & NPI +
The name, address, phone, and NPI of the billing provider or practice. This is where the payment gets sent.
